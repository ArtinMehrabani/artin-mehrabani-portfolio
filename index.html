Publications

Identifying Differentially Methylated Sites for Methylation-Sensitive qPCR-Based NIPT of Trisomies 13 and 18

Title page of the published article outlining the research focus.

Abstract section detailing the methodology and key findings of the study.

Accepted

Journal: Journal of Advanced Biomedical Research (2025)

Abstract: This study presents a significant advancement in non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) by developing a methylation-sensitive quantitative PCR (MS-qPCR) assay for the detection of trisomies 13 and 18. By identifying and validating 22 differentially methylated sites with high trisomy/disomy ratios, we have established a highly specific and reproducible method that serves as a cost-effective and scalable alternative to current next-generation sequencing (NGS) approaches. This research paves the way for more accessible prenatal screening, particularly in resource-limited settings, and future work will focus on validating these findings in cell-free DNA from maternal plasma.

Association Between First Trimester of Pregnancy Medication Use and Fetal DNA Fraction in Maternal Plasma

Title page of the manuscript currently under review, highlighting the research team.

Under Review

Journal: Journal of Ultrasound in Obstetrics and Gynecology

Abstract: This large-scale study, analyzing data from 43,722 pregnant women, investigates the impact of first-trimester medication use on fetal DNA fraction in maternal plasma. Our findings reveal a significant association between the administration of common medications, such as aspirin, enoxaparin, and levothyroxine, and a marked decrease in fetal fraction. These results have critical implications for the accuracy and reliability of non-invasive prenatal screening (NIPS), suggesting that pharmacological exposures should be carefully considered when interpreting test results. This research underscores the need for personalized NIPS protocols that account for maternal medication history.

Supervisor: Assoc. Prof. Kaveh Kavousi, Department of Bioinformatics, University of Tehran

Research Focus: This project focuses on developing and evaluating a range of machine learning models to predict optimal NIPT scheduling. We are exploring classical models such as logistic regression and support vector machines, with plans to advance to more complex ensemble methods and deep learning architectures as needed. The goal is to create a predictive tool that personalizes NIPT scheduling based on maternal clinical variables and fetal fraction dynamics, thereby enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of prenatal screening.
