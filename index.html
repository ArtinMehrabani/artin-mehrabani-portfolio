About

Artin Mehrabani is a dedicated researcher in the field of Biotechnology at the University of Tehran, with a specialized focus on Medical Genetics, Computational Biology, and Non-Invasive Diagnostics. His research endeavors are centered on the development of innovative liquid biopsy techniques and the application of machine learning to enhance personalized medicine. Artin has actively contributed to high-impact projects in non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT), exploring epigenetic markers and feto-maternal data dynamics. With a strong foundation in both wet-lab molecular techniques and dry-lab bioinformatics analysis, he aims to bridge the gap between academic research and translational biotechnology to improve clinical diagnostic accuracy and accessibility.

Publications & Research Projects

Identifying Differentially Methylated Sites for Methylation-Sensitive qPCR-Based NIPT of Trisomies 13 and 18

Published IF: 1.0

Journal: Journal of Advanced Biomedical Research (Dec 2025)

DOI: 10.4103/abr.abr_37_25

Role: Data Curation (equal), Investigation (supporting), Writing – Review & Editing (supporting)

Abstract: This research focuses on identifying specific epigenetic signatures in cell-free DNA to develop a cost-effective, qPCR-based alternative for prenatal screening. By targeting differentially methylated regions, the study provides a robust framework for detecting chromosomal abnormalities with high sensitivity, potentially expanding the reach of NIPT in resource-limited settings.

Association Between First Trimester of Pregnancy Medication Use and Fetal DNA Fraction in Maternal Plasma

Under Peer Review IF: 6.3

Journal: Journal of Ultrasound in Obstetrics and Gynecology (Expected Jul 2025)

Role: Investigation (lead), Conceptualization (equal), Data Curation (equal), Writing – Original Draft (equal)

Research Focus: Investigating the impact of maternal pharmacological exposure on fetal fraction levels. This study analyzes how common medications during the first trimester can influence NIPT reliability, offering critical insights for clinical interpretation and personalized prenatal screening protocols.

Optimizing NIPT Scheduling and Cell-Free Fetal DNA Prediction via Machine Learning Models

In Progress Grant Funded

Supervisor: Assoc. Prof. Kaveh Kavousi, University of Tehran

Role: Investigation (lead), Data Curation (lead), Conceptualization (equal), Writing – Original Draft (equal)

Details: Utilizing classical machine learning models (Pandas, Scikit-learn) to predict optimal scheduling for NIPT based on feto-maternal clinical variables. This project aims to minimize "no-call" results and optimize the timing of prenatal tests through predictive modeling.
